📖 Core Concepts & Definitions

Before we dive in, let's unlock the "ML jargon" used in this project:

Basin A specific region of the ocean where cyclones form (e.g., North Atlantic, West Pacific). Each has its own climate "personality."
Generalisation The AI's ability to see a new ocean it never trained on and still predict accurately by applying universal principles.
Latent Space (z) The AI's "internal imagination." It converts raw data (images/numbers) into a compressed mathematical code that captures the essence of the storm.
Encoder A specialized neural network that translates one type of data (like a 3D weather image) into that internal mathematical code.
Invariance The idea that some things (like the physics of heat rising) work exactly the same way in the Atlantic as they do in the Indian Ocean.
Ablation A scientific "stress test" where we remove one part of the model's brain to see how much worse it gets. It proves if a new feature actually helps.

🧪 The "LOBO" Transfer Strategy

How do we know if our AI is truly "Generalised" and not just memorising one ocean?

Leave-One-Basin-Out (LOBO) Test:
  1. We pick one basin (e.g., South Indian Ocean) to be the "Forbidden Ocean."
  2. We hide it from the AI during training.
  3. The AI trains on the other 5 basins (Atlantic, West Pacific, etc.).
  4. The Moment of Truth: We drop the AI into the South Indian Ocean for the first time. If it predicts well, it has mastered the physics of storms, not just the habits of a single ocean.
Why this matters? As global warming changes current climate patterns, models that only know "habits" will break. Models that know "physics" will survive.

🛠️ The Scientific Audit: Ablation Studies

To prove our brain architecture (PhysIRM) is actually better than standard AI, we run "Scientific Stress Tests."

Type A: Component Ablations (Mind Stress Tests)

We intentionally "dumb down" the model to see if our special features are necessary:

  • No L_phys: We stop grounding the model in real physics parameters. Does it lose its "scientific common sense"?
  • No L_orth: We let the "Physics" and "Environment" brains overlap. Does it get confused between habits and rules?
  • Standard IRM: We use invariant logic but don't give it any physics hints.
  • ERM Baseline: The "Simple AI" approach. No splitting, no physics, just raw data learning.

Type B: Modality Ablations (Senses Stress Tests)

We "blindfold" the model by taking away its input senses:

  • No 3D Images: Can it predict based only on its track history?
  • No Env Context: Can it predict without knowing which month or region it's in?
  • 1D Track Only: The "Minimum Survival" test — predicting only with Lat/Long and Wind speeds.

🏆 The End Goal

Success means achieving a high RI-F1 Score on Unseen Basins.

This means the model can spot the most dangerous, rapidly intensifying storms in parts of the world it has never seen before, potentially saving lives by providing earlier warnings for regions with limited historical data.
